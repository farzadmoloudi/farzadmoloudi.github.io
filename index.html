About

Clinician first, scientist always.

I trained as a physician at Urmia University of Medical Sciences and spent eight years in clinical practice — in emergency medicine and primary care — before moving into full-time research. That clinical grounding shapes how I choose problems: I look for questions that matter at the bedside and pursue them at the bench.

At Massachusetts General Hospital, my work integrates wet-lab experimentation, preclinical small-animal imaging, and computational analysis. I work across cell culture, qPCR, Western blotting, flow cytometry, animal surgery and tumor models, and small-animal MRI — and I apply AI and Python to turn complex imaging datasets into clinically meaningful signal.

Research

What I work on.

Imaging biology

Molecular imaging of cancer

MRI methods that reveal tumor biology — including molecular MRI of dysregulated zinc secretion to detect pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma and track response to targeted therapy.

Bench to bedside

Preclinical models & small-animal MRI

Orthotopic tumor models, animal surgery, and small-animal MR imaging to validate new contrast mechanisms and move discoveries toward translation.

Computation

AI & quantitative image analysis

Python-based pipelines and machine learning for post-processing, multimodal data integration, and biomarker discovery in oncologic imaging.
